How to Use the Salary Calculator

Understanding exactly how your income breaks down is essential for budgeting, negotiating a job offer, or deciding between a salaried position and a freelance hourly rate. Our free Salary Calculator instantly converts your wage across all standard pay periods.

Gross Pay vs. Net Pay

This calculator outputs your Gross Pay. It is vital to understand the difference between gross and net income when planning your finances:

  • Gross Pay: The total amount of money you earn before any taxes, benefits, or deductions are taken out. If you are offered a "$60,000 salary," that is your gross pay.
  • Net Pay (Take-Home Pay): The actual amount of money deposited into your bank account on payday. This is your gross pay minus federal and state income taxes, FICA (Social Security and Medicare), health insurance premiums, and retirement contributions (like a 401k).

Understanding Pay Periods

Depending on your employer, you will likely be paid on one of these common schedules:

  • Weekly: Paid once a week, usually on Fridays. (52 paychecks per year).
  • Bi-Weekly: Paid every two weeks. This is the most common schedule in the US. Because there are 52 weeks in a year, you will receive 26 paychecks. Twice a year, you will experience a "three-paycheck month."
  • Semi-Monthly: Paid twice a month, typically on the 1st and 15th, or the 15th and the last day of the month. (24 paychecks per year).
  • Monthly: Paid once a month. (12 paychecks per year).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How is the hourly wage calculated from a salary?

To find your hourly wage, we first find your total annual salary. We then calculate how many total hours you work in a year (Hours Per Week multiplied by 52 weeks). Finally, we divide your annual salary by your total yearly hours. For a standard 40-hour workweek, you work 2,080 hours a year.

2. What if my job gives me unpaid vacation time?

The standard calculation assumes you are paid for 52 full weeks. If you take two weeks of unpaid vacation, you are only paid for 50 weeks. To adjust for this manually, simply calculate your "Weekly" result and multiply it by the exact number of weeks you plan to work.

3. Is a $50k salary the same as $25 an hour?

Very close! If you work a standard 40-hour week for 52 weeks (2,080 hours total), $25 an hour equals exactly $52,000 a year gross. A flat $50,000 salary breaks down to about $24.04 an hour.
